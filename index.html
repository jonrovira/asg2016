Meet Joji

Born and raised in the Land of Lincoln, Joji is junior studying political science and business institutions. At Northwestern, she has been involved with ASG through a number of different committees as well as senate; this past year, she has been the VP of the Community Relations Committee. As VP, Joji initiated collaboration between Northwestern student groups and Evanston elementary/middle schools, made U-Passes available to NU students on weekends, and helped bring Divvy stations to campus. Outside of ASG, Joji served as President of SCNO, co-founded NU Spring Escape, and was a member of NSH. In her spare time, she auditions (and gets rejected (not butt-hurt or am I?!)) for any and all culinary competition shows.

Meet Archie

Archie was born to Indian immigrants in the United Kingdom, but he grew up in the humble land of the cheese curds (Wisconsin). His long-term dream is to work as a physician and healthcare policy expert on health disparities -- specifically, health disparities affecting people of color and LGBTQ+ people. On campus, Archie has been been on the Community Relations Committee for two years with Joji. He is also the former VP of External Affairs for the South Asian Student Alliance (SASA), and a member of the executive board for Alpha Iota Omicron (AIO), Brown Sugar Acapella, Junior Exec on the Asian-Pacific American Coalition (APAC), Peer Advisor, and Tour Guide in training. Last year, he gave a TEDx talk entitled, “A Tale of Two Melodies” about the intersectionality of Western and Indian culture.

Our Vision

ASG’s goal is to ensure that every Northwestern student has their best possible college experience. But has it really done that?

We don't think so.

It's time for ASG to once again make a difference in each and every student experience at Northwestern. Our paradigm shift will make sure ASG matters to you for longer than election week -- it will ensure it makes an impact on you for your entire Northwestern experience.

The Platform

Part 1: A NU Deal for Student Groups

Streamline Information

We want to create a single website for student groups that contains event publicity, an internal centralized calendar, an advising map, and more.

Reduce Miscommunication

We want to make sure student groups are all on the same page. To accomplish this, we will push for advisor and AE/GE training and the creation of clearer metrics for event success.

Optimize SOFO

We plan on improving the SOFO experience by improving SOFO hours, creating a digital SOFO system, and implementing online advisor approvals.

Part 2: ASG’s NU Deal for you

Social Justice

Anti-blackness, xenophobia, anti-semitism, Islamophobia, homophobia, transphobia, racism, sexism, classism, ableism, and more. We are failing the most vulnerable students of our NU community if we continue to remain negligent of their issues and feelings.

Alcohol and Student Conduct Policy

We believe Northwestern should allow students to develop their sense of personal and collective responsibility by allowing the creation of safe, controlled spaces in which students of legal age may consume alcohol.

Northwestern Judicial Council

The council would serve as both ASG’s highest court and as a hearing panel to address alleged violations of the student conduct standards in order to engender trust in Northwestern's review policies.

Mental Health and Student Wellness

There’s still a negative stigma that surrounds mental health on our campus. Working with CAPS officials and student groups, we want to change our campus’ attitude towards mental health and to continue improving access to mental resources on campus.

Student Athletes

Our administration’s goal is to form an excellent working partnership with the SAAC so that we can better represent a core part of our Northwestern peers who have never fully been integrated into the student government.
